calories

Calories are a unit of measurement for energy content in food and beverages. They represent the amount of energy released when our bodies break down and utilize nutrients. Consuming more calories than needed can lead to weight gain, while consuming fewer calories can result in weight loss. Balancing calorie intake with individual needs is important for maintaining a healthy weight.

Body Mass Index(BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value that indicates the relationship between a person's weight and height. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight (in kilograms) by the square of their height (in meters). BMI provides an estimation of body fatness and is commonly used as a screening tool to assess weight status and associated health risks. BMI values fall into categories such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese, helping to identify potential health concerns related to weight.

Body fat

Body fat percentage refers to the proportion of fat mass in relation to total body weight. It is a measure that indicates the amount of fat stored in the body. Knowing one's body fat percentage can provide insights into overall health, fitness levels, and potential health risks associated with excess body fat. Various methods, such as skinfold calipers, bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA), or DEXA scans, can be used to estimate body fat percentage.

Your water needs

Water is essential for maintaining overall health and well-being. Here are four benefits of water:

  • Hydration: Water is crucial proper for hydration, supporting bodily functions, and maintaining optimal physical performance.
  • Nutrient Absorption: Water helps transport nutrients and oxygen to cells, helping in the absorption and utilization of essential nutrients from food.
  • Body Temperature Regulation: Water helps regulate body temperature through sweat production and cooling mechanisms, preventing overheating.
  • Waste Removal: Adequate water intake supports kidney function, helping in the removal of waste products and toxins from the body through urine production.
